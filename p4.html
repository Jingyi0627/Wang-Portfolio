Artist Statement
My initial visit to an exhibition with a buddy ignited my interest in art. The exhibition's subject focused on human visual bias, and the artists created stunning and surprising pieces using this cognitive trick. I became intrigued because of the wonder that art gave to me at that time. These variously themed pieces are like windows displaying the artist's intended ideas. Even in the most basic tasks, artists seem to have a tender heart.
The language of art is composed of innumerable little volumes, of which graphic design and 3D modeling are only a small portion. People get a sense of excitement and abstraction when they view false three-dimensional graphics on a screen, as though a new universe had materialized before their eyes, in contrast to the real reality. Making graphics is also, in my opinion, a visually engaging hobby. Images, bold inventiveness, text settings, software, typefaces, color coordination, and matching all have an effect and are reflected in one another.
Jingyi Wang jingyi.wang01@sjsu.edu
